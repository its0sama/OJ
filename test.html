Instructions & Theoretical Basis

This instrument integrates three validated frameworks to produce a nuanced picture of your personality architecture. Unlike popular personality quizzes, the questions here draw on empirical research in differential psychology, Jung's theory of psychological types, and the Big Five factor model — the most scientifically replicated personality taxonomy in the literature.

"The shoe that fits one person pinches another; there is no recipe for living that suits all cases." — C. G. Jung, Modern Man in Search of a Soul (1933)

Rate each statement on a 7-point scale reflecting how accurately it describes your habitual patterns of thinking, feeling, and behaviour. There are no correct answers. Respond based on who you are, not who you aspire to be. First impressions tend to be more accurate than deliberated responses.

MBTI / Jung

Measures cognitive function preferences: Extraversion/Introversion, Sensing/iNtuition, Thinking/Feeling, Judging/Perceiving. Derived from Jung's Psychological Types (1921).

Big Five (NEO)

Assesses Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism — the five orthogonal trait dimensions empirically established across cultures (Costa & McCrae, 1992).

Enneagram

Maps core motivational drivers across nine character structures rooted in three instinctual centres: Gut (8–9–1), Heart (2–3–4), Head (5–6–7). Based on Riso & Hudson's Trifix model.

The assessment contains 60 items and takes approximately 12–18 minutes to complete with genuine reflection. Your data is processed entirely within your browser — nothing is transmitted externally.
